Access granted

  • If a man accuses another man and files a complaint of murder against him, but cannot prove it, his accuser will be punished with death.
  • If a lord accuses another lord of witchcraft practices, but he cannot prove them, the person accused of witchcraft will go to the river. If this man has been purified by the river and emerged safe and sound, the person who accused him of witchcraft maneuvers will be punished with death. The one who threw himself into the river will take the property of his accuser.
  • If a man appears in a trial for false testimony and cannot prove the word he has said, if the trial is a capital trial, such a man will be punished with death. If he comes forward to testify about grain or silver, he will suffer the full penalty of this process. He will never again be able to sit with judges in a trial.
  • If a lord steals religious or state property, that lord will be punished with death. Furthermore, the one who received the stolen property from his hands will be punished with death. If the thief has nothing to repay, he will be punished with death. If the seller is the thief he will be punished with death. The owner of the lost item will recover the lost item from him.
  • The buyer will recover from the seller's estate the silver that he had weighed. The owner of the lost thing will recover the lost property from him. If the owner of the lost thing does not present witnesses to testify about the lost object, he is a swindler, he followed a false report, he will be punished with death. If the seller has died, the buyer will take from the seller's house up to five times what he had claimed in this process.
  • If the witnesses of such man are not at hand, the judges will set a period of six months. And if in the sixth month, he does not present his witnesses, this man is a forger. He will suffer the full penalty of this process. If a lord steals the youngest child of another lord, he will receive death.
  • If a lord helps a state slave or a slave of a subaltern or a slave of a subaltern to escape through the great door, he will receive death. If a lord gave refuge in his house to a fugitive male or female slave, belonging to the state or a subordinate, and if he did not hand him over at the call of the crier, the owner of the house will receive death. If a lord seizes a runaway male or female slave in an open field and returns him to his owner, the owner of the slave will give him two shekels of silver. If he retains the slave in his house after the slave is found in his possession, the master will receive death.
  • If the slave flees from the house of the one who captured him, this man will swear by the god to the slave's owner and will go free. If a man breaches a house, he will be killed and hanged in front of the breach. If a man indulges in banditry and is arrested, that man will receive death. If it is a life, the city and the governor will weigh a fortune on its people.
  • If a fire breaks out in a man's house and a man who came to put it out sets his eyes on some property of the owner of the house and appropriates some property of the owner of the house, that man will be thrown into the fire.
  • If an officer or a specialist, while serving in the arms of the king, has been taken prisoner, his son is capable of fulfilling the obligations of the fief, the field and the orchard will be given to him and he will take care of the feudal obligations of his father. Only the one who took charge of them and fulfilled the obligations of the fief will become a feudatory.
  • If a free man broke a bone of another free man, that bone would be broken too.
  • If a son mistreats his father, his hands will be amputated.
  • If anyone steals an ox, ram, pig, donkey, or boat from the temple or the palace, he will pay thirty times the value; If it is a nobleman, ten times the value, and if he does not have the means to pay, he will be guilty of death.
  • If a slave hits a free man on the head, his ear will be cut off.
